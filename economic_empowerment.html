A RICHER INDIVIDUAL IS A STRONGER INDIVIDUAL

It is difficult to talk of empowering without creation of wealth


As we continue with our journey of empowering the marginalized, the main challenge we encounter is scarcity of resources (poverty).It is important for our beneficiaries to know that our role is not to collect donations from the community and pass to them but rather to seek the help of the community to help them grow and become pillars of the society. Our aim is to help women and other vulnerable groups to grow and be able to stand on their own.As we advocate for their rights and remove obstacles from the way, we also give train them on ways to grow economically and support their economic projects.some of the projects undertaken by women for the purpose of growing economically are show-cased below.


POULTRY FARMING

Above, women poultry farming project initiated by WOPA


The project involves rearing of chicken both layers and broilers. Products from this project are sold in local markets and also consumed domestically. In cases where fertilised eggs are produced, chicks that hatch are taken care of and for two to three weeks and then sold.These projects cusion the groups that practise them from dire economic situations and give them time to take care of other needs such as education of children.


CROP FARMING

WOPA encourages and supports women to carry out farming of crops whose market is readily available such as vegetables and fruits.The aim of this is not just selling for money but also increase food security to support nutrition in women,girls and children. Availability of food reduces tension in homes and help to reduce violences in marriage hence alleviating the suffering of women, girls and children. We also aim to make basic needs such as food available so that parents can have plenty of time to focus on other secondary needs such as education of their children and general health of the family.


Vegetable farming project in western kenya


With different women producing different types of crops, Barter trade has become a better mode of operation for women in the same community or same village. Instead of women waiting to sell their crops to get money, they exchange what they have to get what they do not have and live a balanced life. It is now a common phenomenon in local communities to see a woman exchanging cereals such as maize for services such hair dressing. We have also seen women exchange maize with vegetables and other valuable commodities. The important fact here is that families are producing something and using what they produce to get what they do not produce and live a balanced life.

WOPA director inspects crop farming project

PULLING TOGETHER

We mobilize the community to pull together and form groups economic groups that help members to grow together. In groups, women and other marginalized groups can take on more roles and produce more. Working in groups enable members to carry out projects on large scale hence utilizing economy of scale. It is also easier to seek funding from donors and the government when people are working as one team.


Group poultry farming initiative.

WOPA reaches women groups (vyama) and directly get involved in their growth. We provide leadership training on group members and educate them on how to manage funds,enhance trust and legal issues. We also help groups to mobilize resources in a way that enhance growth and development. We help group members to form legal frameworks that guarantees proper running of the group and management of group resources.


WOPA educates economic groups

INNOVATION: THE WAY TO GO

With the current statistics of high unemployment rate in Kenya, self employment is the only way to go. So many youths including girls and young women remain without a job and live below a dollar per day. Our current and future strategy for our girls and women is to encourage innovation. We aim to equip our beneficiaries with technical skills in ICT and other technology so that they can embrace innovation.
