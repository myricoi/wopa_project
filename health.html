EFFECTS OF COVID-19

COVID-19 impacted negatively the effort of Wopa on the life of girl child


Too much happened in the wake of the COVID-19 pandemic that hit the whole globe. In Kenya, the government closed down all schools and learning centres forcing all students to go back to their homes. The Kenyan government also enforced a partial lockdown and in some cases a total lockdown. Schools closed for more than 8 months. Cases of early pregnancy rose vastically with Kakamega county alone recording over 100 cases. This was a negative direction in WOPA's efforts of advocating against teenage pregnancy. During this period, many young girls were married off by parents or themselves.WOPA was and is still actively involved in the fight against the coronavirus pandemic. Through community health volunteers, we were able to educate the community on preventive measures of coronavirus and action to be taken incase of COVID-19 case. We were also actively involved in distribution of face masks.


MOTHER-CHILD HEALTHCARE

Above, WOPA makes health follow-up visits to young mothers and their babies


For any society to achieve its social,cultural and economic dreams, it must be healthy.We consider all aspects of health including physical health, sexual health, reproductive health, psychological health,mental health and nutritional health.Health is key issue for marginalized groups like women and girls.This directly translates to poor health in children born of these mothers leading to vulnerability in children.


WOPA is direcly involved in the healthcare of mothers and their babies, moreso where the mother is a victim of early pregnancy or teenage pregnancy. We make healthcare follow ups on these cases prior to and after delivery of babies. Our interest is to abate complications that may occur before,during or after labour and to ensure that both the baby and the mother lead a healthy life after birth.Most victims of teenage pregnancy tend to flow health procedures that need to be followed during and after pregnancy. This is in some cases due to refusal to accept the situation. In some cases, it is due to stigma and lack of resources.


We mentally nurture young girls who have fallen victims of teenage pregnancy to accept situations and take responsibility.We then ensure that the whole process is seemless and alleviate health risks.Our goal is to have the young girl in good health of body and mind both before and after giving birth. After birth, the young mother needs to be taken care of and rehabilitate so as to as much as possible go back on course where she was prior to falling victim of teenage pregnancy.


WOPA maintains a routine to ensure that the new born goes through all heath protocols such as the vaccination programme from birth to 2 years and the nutrition programme from breast feeding to weaning until the child is capable of feeding on normal food.

HIV/AIDS: INFECTED AND AFFECTED

Stigmatization and victimization of the HIV/AIDS victims is so far the largest challenge to the healthcare of people living with HIV/AIDS. Most infected people confess shying away from routine health procedures through the normal healthcare system in Kenya. It is becoming more and more necessary for the community to come in and champion the are of people living with HIV/AIDS and their affected families/relatives. Proper care for the infected has two advantages: it means giving the victims a longer healthy life to fulfill their dreams and curbing the spread of the virus. In order to slow down the transmission rate, infected people need to live a healthy lifestyle that protects them from deteriorating their own health and protects the rest of the community from getting infected.


WOPA provides Prevention Education and Services- reducing the number of new infections in both vulnerable groups and the general population. Enhancement of the living standards of the people infected and affected by HIV/AIDS: improving home based care services, access to ARV/ART services and protection of PLWHAs Rights. Mitigation of socio-economic impact of HIV/AIDS: adapting existing programmes and developing innovative responses to reduce the impact of the epidemic on communities, social services and economic productivity.

EMPOWERING COMMUNITY HEALTH VOLUNTEERS

Community Health Volunteers have been a single indispensable group when it comes to healthcare service delivery. They work with various organizations including WOPA to deliver healthcare services to the community.Community Health Volunteers are people from the ommunity and so they understand their communities so well than anyone else. They therefore, make delivery of services more effective by ensuring that the service is very specific to the person who really needs it.The importance of this can never be overstated since our resources are limited and it is important to us that these resources only reach those who really need them.


Community Health Volunteer at work. Photo courtesy of UNAIDS.

WOPA has worked and continues to work with community health volunteers in programmes such as anti-malaria campaigns in pregnant mothers and babies, fight of water-born diseases and sanitization programmes. Recently, community health volunteers have helped in distribution of mosquito nets,sanitary pads and dewarming drugs.

SEXUAL HEALTH AND SANITIZATION

Many girls and women living in poverty neglect their sexual/reproduction health and sanitization. Many of them lack the basic knowledge of proper hygiene as far as their sexual and reproduction health is concerned. Even with enough knowledge, financial constraits prevent these women and girls from putting into practice what they know. Many women in have resolved to use of improper material such as matresses as their sanitary material during menstruation. Due to poverty, we have had cases of girls sharing underwears and other personal effects.


WOPA is determined to provide support to girls and women to ensure their sexual,reproduction health and sanitization. We sexual health education to young girls and women. We talk to the community about importance of proper hygiene. We provide sanitary pads and panties from our donors to girls and women.
