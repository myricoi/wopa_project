STATEMENT OF NEED
THE PROBLEM OF MARGINALIZATION

Above, girls from slums receive donations of sanitary pads


The problem of marginalization is a great issue in developing nations across the entire globe. Here in Kenya we have had a history of marginalized groups whose development has been hindered by prejudice and historical disadvantages. Among the most marginalized are women and girls, orphans and vulnerable children and those living with HIV/AIDS. Women and girls cut across all these groups. Whereas these groups have tried with all their efforts to rise out of fade and become vital assets in building the nation, the prevailing conditions have not been so much in their favour. They are always faced with lack of political will to empower them, legal frameworks that disadvantage them, a wild culture that always limits them and lack of economic muscle.

Above,women living in slums.



Many young girls and women are currently living a dangerous lifestyle as a means of survival. They are more and more becoming dependent on their male counterparts to the extent of entering multiple sexual relationships as a means of getting their basic needs. Sometimes, this situation has led to young girls being married of forcefully or willingly getting into marriage so as to have someone to take care of their needs. Cases of school dropout in girls due to early pregnancies are also on the rise.


In communities where girls and women are so much disadvantaged, the issue of vulnerable children is also on the rise. Children are born outside marriages and by young mothers who cannot even handle their own personal needs. The children therefore live a life of malnutrition and without basic needs. Where the child is a girl, chances of the same history repeating with the child are high.


As the society strife to advance and progress, the marginalized seem to take a reverse gear. As towns grow to become cities, the number of women in commercial sex industry also grows. The number of street children sows and HIV cases rise. Without proper strategy to empower girls and women and other marginalized groups, these groups will continue to be vulnerable in spite of the development of the society. HIV/AIDS victims remain to be stigmatized; orphans continue to live a miserable life; women continue to be denied their constitutional rights and girls continue to drop out school to get into early marriages. It is time to empower these groups so that they can rise out of fade against all odds.


The community needs to rise up for the marginalized.
