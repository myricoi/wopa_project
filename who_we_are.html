ABOUT WOPA

OUR MISSION

The mission of WOPA is to eradicate poverty and promote sustainable grass root health and development through community empowerment women and girls involvement.


OUR VISION

WOPA envisions a health society of equity, where all women, girls and other marginalized community groups can realize their full potential to enhance sustainable development.


Girls, women and other marginalized groups have the the right and pontential to become important pillars that support the development of the society;

Every woman has the right to determine the course of her life without fear of any consequenses to ensue..

IN THE BEGINNING..

Above, Women Pillar Alliance Director, Violet Ombaka meets some of the donors in Nairobi Kenya.


Women Pillar Alliance (WOPA) is registered women led non-profit Community Based organization found in 2014 by Violet Ombaka. WOPA operates within Kakamega County but its operation may go beyond the county boundary as may required and requested where need arises. 90% of members are women who are from varied professional background, have Community at Heart and are determined to bring a change in the lives of women and vulnerable people from marginalized areas. The organization is determined in empowering the marginalized through the following interventions that we incorporate:


SEXUAL REPRODUCTIVE HEALTH
We build the capacity of communities to be able to respond to challenges of primary health care within the communities. The focus is on the primary health situation within communities through community mobilization and participatory learning approach.


HIV/AIDS AWARENES
WOPA offer trainings on Prevention, care and support to both infected and affected people through VCT referrals to the nearest hospitals and clinics, condom disbursement, behavior change trainings, home visits, food distribution among others.


OVC SUPPORT
We support needy children by providing home based care as well as catering for their educational needs such as books, uniforms and other study materials.


INCOME GENERATING ACTIVITIES
We involve women in activities that generate income for their sustainability like poultry farming, goat rearing, Banana farming, fish farming, making soaps, growing vegetables and selling.


GIRL CHILD EMPOWERMENT
We advocate for equality of education for both boys and girls We empower girls on their rights since they are at high risk experiencing defilement/rape, gender based violence We provide sanitary towels for adolescent girls to keep them in school For those who have experienced domestic violence, defilement, rape among other vices are provided with counseling For the school drop outs, we provide skills like hair dressing, tailoring among other activities


ADVOCACY/HUMAN RIGHTS (Women Rights, GBV)
We promote self activism by mobilizing women groups for full Participation in the community democracy building, governance and human rights activism. We also utilize local chief assemblies that are held every week to empower women on their rights and men on importance of a woman and respecting her. Facilitate training programs, networking, group management, capacity building and other appropriate training as identified from time to time focusing on promotion of women rights. We organize women and young girls sporting events as a tool for campaigning against Gender based Violence, confidence building and skills development. We advocate for women to own lands and empower them on their rights ( most of widows are denied their property/land rights by the in-laws, throwing her out of her homestead)


NETWORKING AND PARTNERSHIP
We are building partnerships and engage with other institutions as an important strategy in attaining our objectives as an organization. Our aim is to develop and enhance viable partnerships, alliances and networking with various stakeholders among others, foster exchange and sharing knowledge, experiences and innovation.

OBJECTIVES

  • To empower women and other marginalized groups on political, social and economic activities
  • To promote community health and the health of people affected and infected with HIV/AIDs
  • To promote gender equality and advocate for human rights
  • To address the information and learning needs of rural women
  • To promote creativity and innovativeness for women development through establishment of income generating activities (IGAs).
  • To foster behavior change among youths in regard to drug abuse, sex, HIV/AIDS and alcoholism
  • Reinforce common identity and give a chance to the infected to have a sense of belonging.
  • To strengthen alliances and partnerships with relevant bodies and agencies devoted in eradicating poverty among women and promotion of their health
  • To participate and represent WOPA in local, national, regional and international development forums
  • To mobilize both financial and in-kind resources to further development projects of the organization.
  • Create a sense of corporation, integration and unity among women.
  • Promote role of a woman in development of the society.
